faqs:
  - question: "How much does land clearing cost?"
    answer: "Highly variable — it depends on acreage, tree density, access, stump treatment, and haul-off vs. burn. A flat, accessible half-acre residential lot might be a one-day job in the low-to-mid thousands. A multi-acre wooded lot for a building site is a different conversation. We give flat-rate quotes after a site walk."
  - question: "Do I need a permit to clear my own land?"
    answer: "Depends on the jurisdiction, the acreage, and whether the clearing triggers stormwater or sediment control requirements. Maryland's Forest Conservation Act kicks in at certain acreage thresholds. Washington County has its own stormwater rules for disturbed ground. We check your specific situation during the quote and flag anything that needs a permit before we start."
  - question: "Can you grind the stumps too, or just cut the trees?"
    answer: "Both. Standard clearing leaves stumps; we can grind them as part of the same job. If you're building on the lot, stump grinding or full extraction is usually part of the site prep — easier and cheaper to do it all in one mobilization than bring equipment back later."
  - question: "What happens to the wood and brush?"
    answer: "Brush gets chipped on site. Logs get loaded out or stacked if you want to keep them. Burning on site is possible in some jurisdictions with permits and the right weather conditions. We handle all of it as part of the quoted scope."
  - question: "Do you do commercial land clearing?"
    answer: "We handle light commercial and residential building site clearing. Large-scale commercial work (industrial, subdivisions) is outside our typical scope — if you call with a job like that, we'll tell you honestly whether we're the right fit or point you to a crew better suited for the scale."
