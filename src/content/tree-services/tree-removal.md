signsYouNeed:
  heading: "Signs a Tree Might Need to Come Down"
  items:
    - title: "Large dead branches or a dead crown"
      description: "Dead limbs fall without warning. If more than a quarter of the canopy is dead, the whole tree is usually a safety problem."
    - title: "Cracks in the trunk or major limbs"
      description: "Vertical cracks, split forks, or heavy lean after a storm are warning signs a tree is no longer structurally sound."
    - title: "Visible root damage or fungal growth"
      description: "Mushrooms at the base, rotted roots, or a trunk that's hollow at the bottom point to decay you can't see from the canopy."
    - title: "Tree is too close to your house or power lines"
      description: "Proximity alone isn't always a removal reason, but if the tree is damaged AND close to a structure, waiting is the expensive choice."
    - title: "Storm damage you can't repair with pruning"
      description: "If major scaffold limbs are gone and the remaining structure is unbalanced, removal is often safer than hoping it grows back."
